## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Prospect Theory and Heuristics Validity
Tversky & Kahneman (1974): availability, representativeness, anchoring heuristics
produce systematic and predictable judgement errors across populations.
Kahneman & Tversky (1979): prospect theory — loss aversion, diminishing sensitivity,
reference point dependence are stable features of decision under uncertainty.
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED — among the most replicated findings in psychology

### Finding 2: Species-Typical vs Idiosyncratic Defaults
Timberlake & Lucas (1985): default behaviours under uncertainty reflect species-typical
appetitive behaviour patterns (foraging, social affiliation, exploration) before
idiosyncratic conditioning. For human documentary inference: universal defaults
(loss aversion, status quo bias, in-group preference) must be separated from
idiosyncratic conditioned responses unique to the subject.
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for animal behaviour; THEORETICAL for human documentary analogy

### Finding 3: Observable Default Patterns in Documentary Record
Decision records, behavioural histories, and language use all reveal default patterns:
- Loss aversion: asymmetric response to losses vs gains in documented decisions
- Status quo bias: resistance to change observable in decision patterns over time
- Availability heuristic: recent/vivid events disproportionately referenced in reasoning
- Anchoring: first stated position maintained despite contradictory evidence
- In-group bias: cooperation/generosity towards in-group vs out-group asymmetry
Epstein (1994): cognitive-experiential self-theory identifies four dimensions of
experiential processing visible in narrative and behaviour under stress [web:352 by analogy].
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for heuristics identification; EMERGING for documentary application

### Finding 4: Conditioning and Implicit Processes Beyond Dual Process
PMC (2019): conditioning and implicit processes constitute a third system beyond
System 1 and System 2 — historically conditioned responses not captured by either [web:354].
For documentary inference: some default patterns are conditioned responses to specific
stimuli (trauma, formative experiences) not just cognitive heuristics.
CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING — beyond dual process framework

### Finding 5: Contradictions
- Prospect theory replication: some loss aversion findings have not replicated across
  cultures. Nature: EMPIRICAL.
- Heuristics as adaptive vs maladaptive: Gigerenzen et al. argue heuristics are
  ecologically rational, not errors. Nature: PARADIGMATIC.

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validated protocol for quantifying loss aversion parameter from behavioural records.
2. Status quo bias vs genuine preference not distinguishable from documentary sources alone.

## Transferability Verdict
VIABLE — heuristic and loss aversion patterns are directly observable in documented
decisions and language use. Species-typical vs idiosyncratic distinction requires
multiple documented instances, not single observations.
Transfer gap: MINOR for heuristic identification; MODERATE for parameter estimation.

## Failure Mode Register
| Failure Mode | Likelihood |
|---|---|
| Inferring stable defaults from single decision under atypical pressure | LIKELY |
| Confounding strategic loss framing with genuine loss aversion | POSSIBLE |
| Missing conditioned responses by assuming universal heuristic pattern | POSSIBLE |
