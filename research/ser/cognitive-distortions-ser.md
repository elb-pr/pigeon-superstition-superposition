## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Core Taxonomy Validity
Beck (1963): original identification of idiosyncratic cognitive content in depression.
Burns (1980): ten-distortion taxonomy with linguistic examples — the most widely
used clinical operationalisation. Automatic Thoughts Questionnaire (ATQ, Hollon &
Kendall 1980) provides validated item-level examples of distorted cognition in language.
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for clinical context

### Finding 2: Linguistic Surface Forms — Direct Documentary Relevance
European Proceedings linguistic analysis study (2021, N=80 clinical and non-clinical):
automatic thoughts and cognitive distortions identified from natural language speech
samples using grammatical category analysis. Specific distortions map onto specific
syntactic and lexical patterns:
- All-or-nothing: absolute quantifiers (always, never, everyone, no-one)
- Mind reading: certainty about others' mental states without evidence markers
- Fortune telling: unhedged future predictions stated as fact
- Catastrophising: magnitude amplifiers (terrible, disaster, unbearable)
- Personalisation: self-causal attribution for external events [web:339]
CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING — single study, small N, clinical population

### Finding 3: Depression Language and Distortion Patterns
Rude, Gortner & Pennebaker (2004): depression-vulnerable subjects show elevated
first-person singular, reduced positive emotion words, increased negative cognitive
processing words in natural language essays. Consistent with automatic thoughts
and distortion patterns [web:308 by analogy — Pennebaker paradigm].
Psychologytools.com provides operationalised linguistic markers per distortion for
clinical use [web:187]. CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING — application to documentary inference

### Finding 4: Contradictions
- Cognitive distortions vs normal heuristic processing: the distortions described
  by Beck are continuous with normal reasoning shortcuts. Nature: DEFINITIONAL.
- Clinical vs non-clinical: distortion identification in non-clinical subjects risks
  pathologising normal thought. Nature: PARADIGMATIC — important constraint.

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validation study for cognitive distortion inference from documentary sources
   specifically (as opposed to therapy transcripts or speech samples).
2. No inter-rater reliability data for distortion coding from naturalistic text.
3. All-or-nothing and catastrophising are high-frequency in political/persuasive
   rhetoric — context sensitivity not studied.

## Transferability Verdict
VIABLE — distortions have direct linguistic surface forms (absolute quantifiers,
unhedged certainty about others' minds, unqualified future predictions). The 2021
linguistic analysis study is small but methodologically sound.
Transfer gap: MODERATE — clinical populations may express distortions more overtly
than general documentary subjects; context sensitivity required.

## Failure Mode Register
| Failure Mode | Likelihood |
|---|---|
