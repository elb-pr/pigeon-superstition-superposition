## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Hierarchy Validity
Vaillant (1971, 1977, 1992) derived empirical defence hierarchy from 30-year longitudinal
data (Grant Study). Four levels: psychotic, immature, neurotic, mature. Predicts physical
health, relationship quality, and occupational achievement decades later [web:368].
Vaillant, Bond & Vaillant (1986): trained raters coded defences from interview narratives
— first demonstration that defence level predicts 20-year health outcomes [web:82][web:83].
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED

### Finding 2: DMRS — Observer-Rated Instrument (Critical)
Perry (1990) Defense Mechanism Rating Scales (DMRS): 30 defences across 7 levels,
coded by trained raters from verbatim interview transcripts. Does NOT require subject
self-report — designed for observer application from recorded material [web:327].
DMRS-Q (Q-sort version, 2021): further validated, confirms hierarchical structure,
inter-rater reliability ICC=.70–.85 [web:72][web:80].
DMRS-SR-30 (self-report adaptation, 2020): confirms construct alignment with observer
version, but observer version outperforms self-report for immature defences [web:73][web:325].
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED

### Finding 3: Longitudinal / Adaptive Defence Use
Vaillant (2013): mature defences (sublimation, humour, altruism) at midlife predict
physical health and life satisfaction 30 years later. Coded from narrative interviews
by independent raters [web:368].
Emotional control theory (Horowitz, 1988): defence categories map onto observable
patterns in *content and form* of communications — key for documentary inference [web:367].
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED

### Finding 4: Literary and Narrative Application
Multiple studies code defence mechanisms in fictional and biographical narratives using
DMRS or modified schemes [web:366][web:370]. Inter-rater reliability maintained in
non-clinical text contexts (r=.75–.85 with training).
CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING — no formal validation in forensic/intelligence documentary context

### Finding 5: Contradictions
- Defence mechanism construct validity disputed by cognitive-behavioural tradition:
  defences as cognitive schemas vs unconscious processes. Nature: PARADIGMATIC.
- DMRS requires trained coders (40+ hours training) — untrained application produces
  low reliability. Nature: METHODOLOGICAL CONSTRAINT not contradiction.
- Psychotic/immature defences more reliably coded than neurotic/mature from text alone.
  Nature: EMPIRICAL.

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validation study applying DMRS directly to documentary sources (letters, court
   records, social media) — all validation uses therapy transcripts or interviews.
2. No published protocol for coding documentary sources that are not verbatim transcripts.

## Transferability Verdict
VIABLE — DMRS is explicitly designed for observer application from verbatim material.
Transfer gap: MODERATE — validated on therapy transcripts; documentary sources differ
in elicitation context. Immature/psychotic defences more reliably inferred than
neurotic/mature. Trained coder required.

## Failure Mode Register
| Failure Mode | Likelihood |
|---|---|
| Untrained application producing unreliable coding | LIKELY |
| Misidentifying mature defences in polished written output | POSSIBLE |
| Confounding context-appropriate behaviour with defence activation | POSSIBLE |
| Documentary source lacks sufficient affective material to code | POSSIBLE |
